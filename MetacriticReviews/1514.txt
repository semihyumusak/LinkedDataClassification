Attention Deficit Domination goes straight for the doom-rock jugular. [Jan 2012, p.120]
	There's just enough that's worthwhile on this album to hope that Hank3 doesn't fully abandon this concept, but this is a far cry from what he does best, and even serious doom fans are likely to find this is pretty ordinary.
	The album never sounds like the work of a proper band, since there's no actual interplay between any of the instrumental performances.
	ADD is death mental headache. [Nov 2011, p.84]
	Attention Deficit Domination only wishes it could posses as much power as the best doom metal evangelists. There is a noticeable lack of the vacuous bass that propels the best representatives of this genre into hell.
