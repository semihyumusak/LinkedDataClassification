Morgan simply lets his rage rock, infusing the album with the same kind of active/ alt-rock straddling approach that's vaulted Seether's previous releases to gold status.
	With hardly a distraction or ballad in sight, it is their most direct and focused record yet, and quite possibly their most consistent.
	On this South African band's third album, the guitar tones are a teeth-grinding, digitized-sounding nightmare, and a series of I'm-singing-through-a-cell-phone vocal filters can't disguise how played out Morgan's style is.
	Finding Beauty in Negative Spaces is nearly all negative space with very little beauty.
